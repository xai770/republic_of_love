**COMMUNICATION SKILLS:**

* **Written/verbal communication:** The role involves writing formal documentation, contract specifications, and KPI reporting. It also requires clear verbal communication to convey complex technical information to stakeholders without a technical background. Clear and precise language is essential to avoid misunderstandings in contract negotiations and process implementation.

* **Presentation skills:** The Project Lead Contract Compliance/Tech Lead will need to present the proposed process changes and technological solutions to stakeholders, including potentially presenting proposals to senior management or Board members. Effective presentation skills are necessary to persuasively communicate ideas and gain support for project initiatives.

* **Technical translation:** Given that the role involves explaining complex technical processes and issues to stakeholders with varying levels of technical understanding, the ability to translate technical concepts into layman's terms is crucial. This ensures everyone can comprehend the information and contributes to better decision-making.

**INTERPERSONAL SKILLS:**

* **Stakeholder management:** The project lead must manage interactions with diverse stakeholders, including team members, divisional stakeholders, vendors, and external partners. Stakeholder management involves keeping them informed, engaged, and aligned with project goals.

* **Negotiation:** This role requires negotiation skills to establish new processes and agreements that adhere to license conditions. Effective negotiation ensures contracts are structured in a way that benefits both parties while strictly abiding by licensing constraints.

* **Diplomacy:** Diplomatic skills are needed to handle potential conflicts or disagreements, ensuring a peaceful resolution that maintains strong working relationships. This is particularly important when negotiating changes or addressing compliance issues.

* **Conflict resolution:** Skilled at resolving disputes and finding constructive solutions when team members or stakeholders have differing viewpoints on process improvements or technical issues.

**LEADERSHIP & INFLUENCE:**

* **Team leadership:** As a project lead, managing a team is central to ensuring the successful completion of tasks. Leading by example and providing clear direction help to motivate and guide team members.

* **Influence without authority:** The position demands the ability to persuade and influence decisions without direct managerial authority. This includes convincing stakeholders and superiors of the benefits and necessity of proposed changes.

* **Change management:** The project often involves instituting significant process changes, requiring the ability to manage the transition smoothly and ensure the adoption of new practices.

**ANALYTICAL & PROBLEM-SOLVING:**

* **Critical thinking:** Making well-informed decisions requires the ability to analyze situations critically, evaluate the root causes of problems, and consider multiple perspectives before arriving at a solution.

* **Problem decomposition:** Breaking down complex issues into manageable components allows for systematic analysis and tackling of each part individually, ensuring no aspect is overlooked.

* **Root cause analysis:** Understanding why something went wrong is key to preventing similar issues in the future. Root cause analysis helps uncover the fundamental reason behind problems and leads to long-lasting solutions.

**PERSONAL ATTRIBUTES:**

* **Attention to detail:** Precision is critical when reviewing contracts, analyzing data, and ensuring compliance with licensing conditions. Small errors could lead to significant legal or financial issues.

* **Adaptability:** The fast-paced and frequently changing environment of software project management necessitates the ability to pivot strategies and adapt to new circumstances swiftly.

* **Persistence:** This role requires tenacity, especially in negotiating complex agreements or convincing stakeholders to adopt new practices. Persistence through challenges ensures that projects are completed successfully.

* **Political savvy:** Understanding organizational politics is essential for navigating the internal landscapes of large organizations, securing buy-in from key players, and managing internal relationships effectively.
