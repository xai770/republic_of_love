## Card polish — Perplexity-inspired refinements

### Comparison: Perplexity pricing cards vs. talent.yoga search cards

**What Perplexity does well:**
- **Thin borders:** ~1px, barely visible grey (`#e5e7eb`-ish). Cards barely "have" a border.
- **Off-white page background:** The page behind the cards isn't pure white — it's a warm grey, so white cards *float* above it naturally.
- **Almost no answered/active glow:** The "Max" card has a very thin teal/cyan left-accent border. No thick coloured outlines, no double-ring `box-shadow`.
- **Premium whitespace:** Cards breathe. No heavy element crowds the eye.
- **Minimal shadow:** Just enough to lift the card, never enough to notice.

**Where talent.yoga is heavier:**
- 2px border + 2px green answered ring = visually loud "selected" state
- `box-shadow: 0 0 0 1px #16a34a` on answered = double green outline
- Pure white page background means white cards have zero contrast — they blend into the page
- The green is nice but thick borders make it feel like a dashboard, not a guided experience

### Plan

| # | Change | Detail |
|---|--------|--------|
| 1 | Shade the card zone background | `.situation-container` gets `#f8fafc` bg + slight rounding |
| 2 | Thin card borders | 2px → 1px on front + back |
| 3 | Soften answered state | Remove green `box-shadow` ring; keep thin 1.5px green border |
| 4 | Lighten default border colour | `#e2e8f0` → `#e5e7eb` (Perplexity-weight) |
| 5 | Refine hover | Lighter shadow, no blue border overshoot |
| 6 | Dark mode counterpart | Shade zone → `#111827`, keep existing dark card colours |
