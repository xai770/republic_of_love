### 21:58 — Evening: step 11 redesign + bug fixes

Finished the day with step 11 (notification opt-in) and a run of small polish fixes.

- **Step 11 fully redesigned** (`db05fc0`) — replaced the old title/subtitle/two-button
  layout with a proper four-paragraph body explaining the privacy model, then a single
  dynamic continue button that reads:
  - "Weiter, ich möchte nicht benachrichtigt werden" (no email entered)
  - "Weiter, ich möchte benachrichtigt werden" (valid email typed)
  Label switches live on `oninput` via a simple regex email check.
  All three COPY variants (en, de_du, de_sie) rewritten.
  Card widened from 540px → 660px to fit the longer text.
  Also fixed a pre-existing dead bug: notification JS was referencing `#s10-*` IDs
  instead of `#s11-*`, so the feature had never actually worked.

- **`brandify` scope fix** (`ce128fc`) — step 11 body rendering called `brandify()`
  from inside `applyText()`, but it was only defined as a `const` inside two nested
  `if` blocks (step 2 render and `previewLang`). ReferenceError → entire `goTo()`
  chain died mid-fade, freezing the card. Fixed by hoisting `brandify` to IIFE level.
  This also broke the flag buttons on step 1 — both fixed in one commit.

- **Stuck spinner on back-from-step-9** (`94714b2`) — second instance of the confirm
  button getting stuck. On success, `completeOnboarding()` called `goTo(9)` but never
  restored the button it had just disabled+spun. Coming back from step 9 to step 8
  found it still frozen. Two-part fix: restore button before `goTo(9)` on success path;
  call `_resetConfirmButtons()` in `goTo()` whenever the target is step 8.

- **Step 9 skip button** (`30db5ae`) — `btn-ghost` → `btn-secondary` so it matches
  the ← Zurück button visually. No more underlined orphan.

- **Step 10 settings note above buttons** (`69fef07`) — "These can be changed anytime
  in Settings." moved from below the button row to between the dropdowns and buttons.

- **Step 11 email copy** (`b223261`) — last paragraph softened to mention the setting
  is changeable anytime in Settings, removing the slight pressure of the original phrasing.

### 2026-02-24 — Tier rename: Guest / Member / Sustainer

Decision: the three participation tiers are now called **Guest**, **Member**, and **Sustainer**.

- **Guest** (was: Free) — warm and honest; implies a natural social progression to membership
- **Member** (was: Standard) — signals belonging and contribution, not a product grade
- **Sustainer** — unchanged

German: **Gast** / **Mitglied** / **Unterstützer** (Unterstützer unchanged)

Naming alternatives considered: Standard (too mediocre), Pro (SaaS vocabulary), Free Member / Full Member (redundant), Free / Member / Sustainer (still cold). Guest / Member / Sustainer chosen.
